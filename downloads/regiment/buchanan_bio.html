Biographies

Lieutenant-Colonel Cameron Buchanan, CD - Commanding Officer  

Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan was recruited into the regular force in 1976 and after basic military training, joined the 1st Battalion, The Royal Canadian Regiment, in London Ontario as a Platoon Commander. After a tour of duty with 1st Battalion he was attached posting to the Canadian Airborne Regiment in Petawawa Ont. He left the Airborne Regiment in 1979 to get married and thereafter was posted to Army HQ ( FMC) in St Hubert Quebec. He worked on several projects and exercise plans that included RV 81 (Gage town N.B) and RV83 (Wainwright AB). In 1984 he was posted to 3 RCR in Winnipeg Manitoba and served as Recce Platoon commander and as a Company 2i/c. During this time Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan served as the Battalion Ops Officer during the UNFCYP tour in Cypress 1986.

In 1987 he was posted to CFE HQ in Lahr Germany where he served in the G-1 branch. He returned to Canada for a six month period to complete the CLFSC in Kingston and returned as the 1Cdn Div (Forward) HQ as the Div Plans Officer. After 1 year in this position he re-joined 3RCR as a Company Commander when the unit returned to Baden-Sollingen Germany. It was during this time that Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan and half of the unit was assigned to the 1st Bn Royal 22ieme Regiment as the first Canadian Peacekeeping unit to the former Yugoslavia. He received the Governor General’s citation for his part in the opening of Sarajevo airport in July 1992.

Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan and his family were posted directly from Germany to the CFCSC College in Toronto in 1993. After completed the Staff College, he was posted back to Europe as the training advisor to the School of Infantry in Warminster U.K. The School was closed in 1995 and Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan was responsible for transferring and starting up the Basic Infantry Training course in Cattrick, North Yorkshire. Following successful completion of the start up operation in Cattrick, Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan was returned to Canada to be DCO of the 1st Battalion, royal Canadian Regiment. During this time he was also assigned the job of developing the LFCA Reserve Evaluation system in Meaford Ontario. It was during this period that he was selected for University training at the University of Manitoba.

Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan and his family moved to Winnipeg in 1997 where he completed his Baccalaureate in 1999. He was then posted as the LFWA Domestic Operations Detachment Commander in Winnipeg. Following this tour he was posted to 38 CBG HQ as the G-3.

Lieutenant-Colonel Buchanan retired from the regular force and took a federal position within the newly formed Ministry of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness Canada as the Assistant Regional Director for Manitoba. He transferred to the Reserve Force the same year and has served as the OC of A Company, DCO and Acting CO of the Queen’s Own Cameron Highlanders of Canada before assuming command of the Regiment in October 2005. He has been married for over 25 years to Valerie (Fleming) and has one son Arthur.
