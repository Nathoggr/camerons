Biographies

Major Brett Takeuchi, CD - Deputy Commanding Officer  


In April of 1997 Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi was seconded to 38 Canadian Brigade Group Headquarters where he filled the position of Chief Instructor.  He completed the Militia Command and Staff Course in July 1999.  During the preparations for Op ABACUS, Lieutenant-Colonel Major Takeuchi was appointed G6 for the Provincial Task Force Headquarters. 

Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi returned to the Camerons in January 2000 and was promoted to the rank of Major that October.  He completed the UN Military Observers Course at the Peace Support Training Centre and subsequently served as Deputy Team Leader of an observer team in Sierra Leone from June to December 2001. 

Upon return to regimental duties, he met and married his wife Cristina (Avram), formerly of Buzau, Romania.  Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi assumed the position of Coordinator for the Winnipeg and Region Dispute Resolution Centre in April of 2002 and served in that capacity until July 2005. 

In August 2005 Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi returned solely to Regimental duties, and assumed the position of Deputy Commanding Officer of the Regiment.  Attach-posted to 1 Canadian Air Division, he filled the position of Headquarters Services Officer while continuing to parade on voluntary service with the Camerons from June to November 2006.  He is a graduate of the Canadian Forces College, having completed the Joint Reserve Command and Staff Program in July 2007.   

On 08 November 2008, Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi was promoted to his current rank and appointed Commanding Officer of the Winnipeg Infantry Tactical Grouping which comprises The Royal Winnipeg Rifles and The Queen’s Own Cameron Highlanders of Canada.  Lieutenant-Colonel and Mrs Takeuchi have two sons, Graeme and Cameron.

 

The son of Sumio and Ann Takeuchi, Lieutenant-Colonel Brett Takeuchi was born in Winnipeg, MB on October 16, 1968.  He joined The Queen's Own Cameron Highlanders of Canada in July 1988 as a private soldier.  He attended the University of Winnipeg where he graduated with a Bachelor's degree in History.  Lieutenant-Colonel Takeuchi was accepted for the RESO program in the spring of 1990 and was commissioned as an infantry officer that September.
